# Future Research Plans - Video Script (6-7 Minutes)

**Duration:** ~6-7 minutes (approximately 950-1000 words at conversational pace)

---

## OPENING (30 seconds)

Hello, I'm Soham Poddar. I recently completed my PhD from IIT Kharagpur, and today I want to share my vision for the future of AI research—a future where artificial intelligence is not just powerful, but *sustainable*.

Every time you ask ChatGPT a question, it consumes energy. Multiply that by billions of queries daily, and we have a growing environmental crisis hiding in plain sight. My research asks: *Can we make AI smarter about how it uses energy?*

---

## THE PROBLEM (45 seconds)

The AI community has achieved remarkable progress, but at a cost. Large Language Models have shifted the computational burden from training—which happens once—to inference—which happens continuously, billions of times a day.

Here's what concerns me: Current AI systems treat every query the same. Ask a simple factual question, and you get the same computational treatment as a complex reasoning task. It's like using a sledgehammer to crack a nut.

And there's a deeper issue. Through my recent research published at ACL and NAACL 2025, I discovered that LLMs generate unnecessarily verbose responses. They pad simple answers with redundant information—consuming 2 to 3 times more energy than necessary.

This is what I call "Red AI"—performance at any cost. My research proposes a shift to "Green AI"—where efficiency is a first-class citizen.

---

## MY CORE INSIGHT (30 seconds)

Here's my central thesis: *The most energy-efficient computation is the one you don't perform.*

Current approaches focus on making tokens faster—through better hardware, quantization, and optimized inference. But I ask a different question: *How do we generate the right tokens—minimizing output while maximizing utility?*

This "semantic efficiency" perspective opens entirely new optimization dimensions that complement hardware improvements.

---

## RESEARCH DIRECTIONS (2 minutes)

Let me outline my key research directions.

**First, Cognitive Economy.** I want to teach AI models to value conciseness. Through what I call "Energy-First Preference Optimization," we can train models to prefer informative but brief responses. My preliminary work shows we can achieve 30 to 50 percent token reduction without losing information quality.

**Second, Adaptive Inference.** Not all queries need the same computational power. I propose a "System 1 / System 2" routing approach—simple queries go to lightweight models, complex reasoning escalates to larger ones. Early-exit mechanisms can terminate generation when the informational need is already met. This alone could yield 40 percent efficiency gains at the cluster level.

**Third, Sustainable Agentic AI.** This builds on my current work at HPE Labs. LLM agents—systems that plan, use tools, and iterate—can consume 10 to 100 times more energy than single queries. Yet their energy footprint is completely unstudied. I want to develop agents that work within "energy budgets" and can run on edge devices using small language models.

**Fourth, AI for Social Good.** Efficiency isn't just about saving energy—it's about democratizing access. I want to build models small enough to run offline on basic smartphones—10 megabyte models maintaining 95 percent accuracy. This enables health chatbots in areas with unreliable connectivity, disaster response systems that work when infrastructure fails, and misinformation detection at massive scale with limited resources.

**Finally, I propose GreenEval**—a standardized benchmark for AI sustainability. We need common metrics like "Joules at Quality" and "Information per Watt." I envision an "AI Energy Star" labeling system that brings transparency to model efficiency.

---

## TEACHING & MENTORING (1 minute)

I'm prepared to teach both core courses—like Programming, Algorithms, and Operating Systems—and advanced electives in NLP, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Information Retrieval and Large Language Models. But I also want to introduce two new courses that don't exist anywhere in India yet.

The first is **Sustainable AI and Green Computing**—covering energy-efficient machine learning, carbon-aware computing, and responsible AI deployment. The second is **AI Agents and Autonomous Systems**—focusing on modern agentic frameworks, tool use, and planning.

My teaching philosophy emphasizes project-based learning. I want students building real systems that solve real problems—not just passing exams. I also believe we must teach students how to use AI tools ethically and effectively, preparing them for a world where AI assistance is ubiquitous.

For mentoring, I believe in gradual independence—starting with guided projects and progressively increasing autonomy. My goal is to produce researchers who can lead their own research programs, whether in academia or industry.

---

## COLLABORATIONS & INSTITUTION BUILDING (1 minute)

Given the fundamental requirement for sustainability, I see tremendous opportunities for collaboration.

Within IIT Kharagpur, I want to work with existing research groups—collaborating on content governance and social media analysis, partnering on edge-deployable IoT systems, and contributing to the AI Department's interdisciplinary initiatives in healthcare, law, and climate.

I also bring industry connections. My current work at HPE Labs, and prior collaborations with Accenture have shown me how to translate research into practice. I want to establish partnerships focused specifically on sustainable AI, helping companies reduce their AI infrastructure's carbon footprint.

Beyond individual projects, I aim to build community. I've organized shared tasks at ICPR, FIRE, and other venues. I want to continue hosting workshops, developing open-source tools, and creating datasets that advance the field.

---

## WHY INDIA, WHY NOW (30 seconds)

India presents a unique opportunity. Coal generates 70 percent of our electricity—meaning AI growth directly increases emissions. Yet AI must serve 1.4 billion people, many with limited hardware.

Despite significant AI investments, sustainability remains unexplored. I want to change that. IIT Kharagpur, with its strong research groups and institutional commitment to sustainability, is the ideal place to pioneer this work.

---

## CLOSING (30 seconds)

The trajectory of AI is currently unsustainable. But I don't propose halting progress—I propose *smarter* progress.

My vision has three pillars: research excellence in sustainable AI, teaching the next generation to think efficiency-first, and building institutions and partnerships that translate research into impact.

I'm committed to demonstrating that efficiency and capability advance together—and to training researchers who will build AI systems that are not just powerful, but responsible.

Thank you.
