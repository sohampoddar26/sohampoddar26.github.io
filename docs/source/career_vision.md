## 2. Research Vision

### 2.1 The Problem

The rapid proliferation of Large Language Models has shifted AI's computational burden from training to **inference**—a continuous operational expenditure powering billions of daily interactions. This research program proposes a paradigm shift from **"Red AI"** (performance-at-any-cost) to **"Green AI"** (efficiency-as-a-priority).

**Why India and IIT Kharagpur must Lead This Research:**

- AI serving 1.4 billion people requires extraordinary efficiency. Many deployments must work with limited hardware. 
- Coal comprises ~70% of India's electricity; AI growth directly increases emissions.
- Despite significant AI investment (AI4Bharat, Sarvam AI) in India, **AI sustainability remains underexplored**

**Why IIT Kharagpur is Ideally Positioned:**

- The **Department of Computer Science** has strong research groups in **AI/ML, NLP, Social Computing and Hardware Systems** provide natural collaboration opportunities
- The **Department of Artificial Intelligence** creates opportunities for pioneering sustainable AI for helping society.
- IIT Kharagpur's "Sustainability@IIT Kharagpur" initiative demonstrates institutional commitment to sustainable development
- Established government and industry partnerships enable translation of research.

### 2.2 Research Directions

#### **Direction 1: Cognitive Economy & Semantic Efficiency**

- Define "Information Density"—useful information per token/per joule
- Develop **Energy-First Preference Optimization (EPO)** training
- Create new metrics: **"Joules @ Quality" (J@Q)**, "Information-per-Watt"
- **Target**: 30-50% token reduction without quality loss

#### **Direction 2: Adaptive Inference & Intelligent Routing**

- **System 1/2 Routing**: Route simple queries to small models, complex reasoning to large models
- **Early-Exit Mechanisms**: Terminate generation when informational need is met
- **Dynamic Model Cascading**: Try small models first, escalate if confidence is low
- **Target**: 40% cluster-level efficiency gain

#### **Direction 3: Post-Transformer Architectures**
- Develop **Hybrid Mamba-Transformer "Chimera"** models (90% Mamba / 10% Attention)
- Investigate **BitNet-style 1.58-bit quantization** with mixed-precision routing
- **Target**: 60% energy reduction for long-context tasks

#### **Direction 4: Sustainable Agentic AI**
*Leveraging current experience at HPE Labs on agentic frameworks*

- Characterize energy consumption across agentic frameworks (LangGraph, AutoGen, CrewAI)
- Develop energy-aware planning with "energy budgets"
- Enable **edge-deployable agents** using Small Language Models
- **Target**: Agents achieving goals with 10x fewer LLM calls

#### **Direction 5: AI for Social Good Applications**
*Building on my PhD work on health misinformation and the MESSAGE CHECK system*

- Energy-efficient misinformation detection at billion-post scale
- Edge-deployable health chatbots for developing regions
- Disaster response NLP on emergency infrastructure
- Future expansion: agriculture, climate monitoring, mental health support, children learning
- **Target**: 10MB models maintaining 95% accuracy, deployable offline

#### **Direction 6: Carbon-Aware Infrastructure**
- Develop carbon-aware schedulers routing jobs to low-carbon regions/times
- Implement temporal load shifting strategies
- **Target**: 20-40% carbon reduction through intelligent scheduling

### 2.3 Proposed Benchmark: GreenEval

To address the lack of standardized sustainability metrics, I propose developing **GreenEval**:
- **Hardware Profiles**: Standardized GPU configurations, edge device benchmarks
- **Task Suite**: 10,000+ prompts with gold-standard concise answers
- **Metrics**: Joules @ Quality (J@Q), tokens/Joule, Information-per-Watt
- **"AI Energy Star" Labels**: Training emissions, inference energy, efficiency grades (A-F)

**New Courses I Would Introduce:**

### 3.2 Pedagogical Approach

- **Project-Based Learning**: Students work on real-world problems with societal impact, with emphasis on building working systems, not just theory
- **AI in Assignments and Learning**: Integrate the use of modern AI tools into assignments, teaching students how to leverage these tools ethically and effectively while maintaining academic integrity and critical thinking.
    - **Industry/Academia Exposure**: Guest lectures from practitioners
- **Research Integration**: Introduce cutting-edge research papers in advanced courses

### 3.3 Student Mentoring Philosophy

Drawing from my experience mentoring junior researchers (UG, PG, early PhD) and organizing shared tasks, I believe in:

- **Gradual Independence**: Start with guided projects, progressively increase autonomy
- **Collaborative Environment**: Foster peer learning and team research
- **Industry Readiness**: Balance academic rigor with practical skills
- **Publication Focus**: Guide students toward high-quality conference and journal publications
- **Holistic Development**: Encourage participation in competitions, workshops, and community building

## 6. Summary: Why IIT Kharagpur

My career vision centers on three pillars:

- **Research Excellence**: Pioneer Sustainable AI in India—reducing AI's environmental footprint while improving capability through semantic efficiency, adaptive inference, and carbon-aware systems.

- **Teaching & Mentoring**: Train efficiency-conscious AI researchers—building human capital for responsible AI development through innovative courses, project-based learning, and research mentorship.

- **Institution Building**: Establish IIT Kharagpur as a Green AI hub—positioning the institute at the forefront of sustainable computing globally through the Sustainable AI Lab (SAIL), industry partnerships, and policy engagement.

**Why I Wish to Return to IIT Kharagpur:**

IIT Kharagpur shaped my research identity. As a place that gave me my MTech (Rank 2), my PhD under exceptional mentors, and the PMRF fellowship, returning as a faculty member represents not just a career goal but a commitment to giving back. The department's world-class research environment, the new AI Department's growth trajectory, the institute's sustainability commitment, and the opportunity to continue collaborating with mentors and colleagues make IIT Kharagpur the ideal home for my research vision.

The trajectory of AI is currently unsustainable. My research proposes a necessary correction—not by halting progress, but by pursuing **smarter advancement**. By teaching models cognitive economy and developing energy-aware systems, we can dramatically reduce environmental impact while often *improving* quality.

I am committed to building a research program at IIT Kharagpur that demonstrates **efficiency and capability advance together**—and to training the next generation of researchers who will carry this vision forward.
