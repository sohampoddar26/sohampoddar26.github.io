As an alumnus of IIT Kharagpur (MTech 2020, PhD 2025), I seek to return to my alma mater to establish India as a pioneer in Sustainable Artificial Intelligence research. Modern Generative AI  My vision centers on a paradigm shift from "Red AI" (performance-at-any-cost) to "Green AI" (efficiency-as-a-priority), addressing one of AI's most pressing challenges: the unsustainable energy consumption of Large Language Models during inference.

## Research Vision ##

My research program addresses the critical gap in AI sustainability through six interconnected directions:

**1. Cognitive Economy & Semantic Efficiency**: Building on my ACL and NAACL 2025 publications, I aim to develop "Information Density" metrics and Energy-First Preference Optimization (EPO) training to achieve 30-50% token reduction without quality loss.

**2. Adaptive Inference**: Implementing System 1/2 routing, early-exit mechanisms, and dynamic model cascading to route queries intelligently—simple questions to small models, complex reasoning to large ones.

**3. Efficient Architectures**: Developing hybrid Mamba-Transformer models and investigating 1.58-bit quantization for dramatic energy reductions in long-context tasks.

**4. Sustainable Agentic AI**: Leveraging my HPE Labs experience to characterize and reduce energy consumption in agentic frameworks, targeting 10x fewer LLM calls while maintaining effectiveness.

**5. AI for Social Good**: Creating edge-deployable models (10MB, 95% accuracy) for misinformation detection, health chatbots, and disaster response—extending my PhD work on health communication systems.

**6. Carbon-Aware Infrastructure**: Building schedulers that route computational jobs to low-carbon regions and times, achieving 20-40% carbon reduction.

To standardize this emerging field, I propose **GreenEval**—a benchmark suite with hardware profiles, task suites, and "AI Energy Star" labels for transparency.

## Why IIT Kharagpur ##

IIT Kharagpur is ideally positioned for this vision: strong AI/ML research groups, the newly established AI Department, institutional commitment to sustainability, and robust industry partnerships. India's unique challenges—serving 1.4 billion people with limited infrastructure and coal-dependent electricity—make sustainable AI not just desirable but essential.
However, despite significant investments in AI, sustainability remains underexplored.

My career vision demonstrates that efficiency and capability advance together. I am committed to training the next generation of researchers who will pioneer smarter, greener AI systems.
